Where things stand

The problem is not what you have built.

What you have developed across housing, energy, nanocellulose, and land rejuvenation is inspiring. Independently validated, internationally recognised, and directly solving problems that government and industry have talked around for decades.

And yet. The funding picture does not yet reflect that. Work of this complexity rarely lands well without a deliberate strategy behind it. Multi-initiative, community-led, systems-level work does not fit neatly into government funding categories, and that friction shows up in ways that are not always easy to see from the inside.

Before I can tell you what is getting in the way, I need to understand the full picture. That is what Phase 1 is for.

What this proposal addresses

Before we search for funding, we build the foundation that makes that search worthwhile. Three phases: Strategic Foundations, The Build, and a Quarterly Grant Scout. Built around your work, not around what the databases happen to list.

The approach

Your initiatives are not separate projects. They are one ecosystem.

Housing that communities can build themselves. Renewable energy that keeps that housing powered. Nanocellulose that turns farm and forestry waste into structural materials and hospital-grade antimicrobials. Land rejuvenation that makes degraded country productive again. Each of these is compelling on its own. Together, they form something far more significant: a vertically integrated, community-led model for how disadvantaged regions can become self-sufficient.

The funders who will be most interested in your work are not the ones looking at housing in isolation, or energy, or materials science. They are the ones looking for systems change. That is exactly what you have built. Our job is to map the ecosystem from the inside and find the funders who see it.

The ecosystem is the competitive advantage.

Everything that has worked against you so far, the complexity, the interconnectedness, the fact that your work does not sit in any single funding category, is precisely what makes it compelling to the right funder. Phase 1 is where we build the language and the strategy to make that visible.

Phase One  ·  One-off Project

Strategic Foundations

Before we build a funding strategy worth following, we need to know where you have been and where you are. Phase 1 produces the foundational documents and strategic plan that make everything else count.

01

Deep Discovery

A session covering the full ecosystem: housing, energy, nanocellulose, and land rejuvenation. The science, the testing results, the partnerships, the evidence base, the budgets, the goals. I need to understand how the pieces connect before I can represent the work to funders.

02

Funding History Review

A review of past applications and funding attempts: what was applied for, what happened, and why. We look for patterns, gaps in positioning or eligibility, and opportunities left on the table.

03

Grant Readiness Gap Analysis

An honest assessment of where the organisation sits right now relative to what competitive funders expect to see. This covers governance documentation, evidence base, co-contribution capacity, impact data, and narrative coherence. For each gap identified, you get a clear action to close it before the right opportunity opens.

04

Theory of Change, Mission and Vision

Establishing the narrative that sits beneath everything: what change you are trying to create, how your work creates it, and what success looks like. The bedrock of every compelling application, and what stops you being misread.

05

Strategic Funding Plan

From everything gathered: the funding landscape most relevant to your work, the funders worth pursuing (Australian and international), the sequencing, and the preparatory steps for each priority application.

How this works

Phase 1 is a single, fixed-fee project. Complete when we have foundational documents and a strategic plan you can act on. The deliverables are yours. No obligation to continue.

Phases Two and Three  ·  Concurrent

Closing gaps and building the pipeline. At the same time.

Once Phase 1 is complete, two workstreams begin together. Phase 2 uses the gap analysis to build the readiness infrastructure your applications will depend on. Phase 3 starts the quarterly intelligence cycle so the funding landscape is already being mapped while that work progresses.

Running them in parallel means that by the time a strong opportunity lands in the quarterly report, you are ready to act on it.

Phase Two

The Build

Every gap identified in Phase 1 becomes a specific action here. The scope is shaped by what Phase 1 surfaces, but typically covers business structure decisions (including whether NFP registration is the right move), strategic positioning, and a communications strategy. From there we build out the documentation layer: governance, evidence packaging, funder-facing narratives for each initiative, and budget narrative. The deliverables from this phase are what your applications will stand on.

Phase Three

Quarterly Grant Scout

The intelligence cycle begins in parallel, so the funding landscape is already being mapped while The Build progresses. Four times a year: a curated opportunity report with green, amber, and red eligibility ratings across Australian foundations, government programs, and international funders, followed by a 60-minute strategy call to review and prioritise. Priority grant writing access at a discounted rate. After the 5-month project, the Scout continues as an ongoing quarterly retainer.

On grant writing

Grant writing fees are separate and quoted per application based on complexity and length. The Scout retainer means you only commit to writing fees when eligibility, evidence, and fit are confirmed.

The investment

Five months to foundations. Quarterly from there.

The 5-month project covers all three phases at a flat rate of $1,500 per month. Phases 2 and 3 run concurrently once Phase 1 is complete. After month 5, the Quarterly Grant Scout continues as an ongoing retainer.
