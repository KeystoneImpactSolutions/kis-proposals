Where things stand

The problem is not what you have built.

What you have developed across housing, energy, nanocellulose, and land rejuvenation is inspiring. Independently validated, internationally recognised, and directly solving problems that government and industry have talked around for decades.

And yet. The funding picture does not yet reflect that. Work of this complexity rarely lands well without a deliberate strategy behind it. Multi-initiative, community-led, systems-level work does not fit neatly into government funding categories, and that friction shows up in ways that are not always easy to see from the inside.

There is also a dimension of this work that changes the funding landscape considerably. My Mob Enterprises is Indigenous-led. That opens a specific set of funding streams, from government Indigenous affairs programs through to philanthropic pools designed for First Nations-led economic and community development, that require their own deliberate strategy to access well.

What this ecosystem needs is not just a list of grants. It needs someone who takes ownership of the full funding pipeline: the strategy, the readiness work, and the writing. That is what this proposal is for.

What this proposal covers

One partnership, two phases. The first builds what every strong application depends on: a complete picture of the ecosystem, the readiness gaps, and the strategic plan. The second is where I take ownership of the full funding pipeline, from opportunity identification through to submission-ready applications.

The approach

Your initiatives are not separate projects. They are one ecosystem.

Housing that communities can build themselves. Renewable energy that keeps that housing powered. Nanocellulose that turns farm and forestry waste into structural materials and hospital-grade antimicrobials. Land rejuvenation that makes degraded country productive again. Each of these is compelling on its own. Together, they form something far more significant: a vertically integrated, community-led model for how disadvantaged regions can become self-sufficient.

The funders who will be most interested in your work are not the ones looking at housing in isolation, or energy, or materials science. They are the ones looking for systems change. That is exactly what you have built. Before we can represent it to those funders, we need to map it from the inside and build the language to make it visible. That is the starting point.

The ecosystem is the competitive advantage.

Everything that has worked against you so far, the complexity, the interconnectedness, the fact that your work does not sit in any single funding category, is precisely what makes it compelling to the right funder. Building the case for it, and then pursuing it with a dedicated partner, is what this engagement is designed to do.

The Strategic Funding Partnership  ·  Phase One

Strategic Foundations

Before we pursue any opportunity, we build what every strong application depends on: a complete picture of where you have been, where you are, and where the funding landscape intersects with what you have built. Phase One produces the foundational documents and strategic plan that the full partnership runs on.

01

Deep Discovery

A session covering the full ecosystem: housing, energy, nanocellulose, and land rejuvenation. The science, the testing results, the partnerships, the evidence base, the budgets, the goals. I need to understand how the pieces connect before I can represent the work to funders.

02

Funding History Review

A review of past applications and funding attempts: what was applied for, what happened, and why. We look for patterns, gaps in positioning or eligibility, and opportunities left on the table.

03

Grant Readiness Gap Analysis

An honest assessment of where the organisation sits right now relative to what competitive funders expect to see. This covers governance documentation, evidence base, co-contribution capacity, impact data, and narrative coherence. For each gap identified, you get a clear action to close it before the right opportunity opens.

04

Theory of Change, Mission and Vision

Establishing the narrative that sits beneath everything: what change you are trying to create, how your work creates it, and what success looks like. The bedrock of every compelling application, and what stops you being misread by funders who do not yet have the language for what you have built.

05

Strategic Funding Plan

From everything gathered: the funding landscape most relevant to your work, the funders worth pursuing across Australian and international programs, the sequencing, and the preparation steps for each priority opportunity. This is the document the partnership runs on.

How Phase One works

Phase One is the opening phase of the partnership. When the foundational documents and strategic plan are complete, Phase Two begins. The deliverables are yours throughout.

The Strategic Funding Partnership  ·  Phase Two

This is what it looks like when funding becomes someone else's job.

Phase Two is the embedded partnership in action. I become your funding strategist, responsible for the full pipeline from the moment an opportunity is identified to the moment a submission-ready application lands in your inbox. You focus on the work. I own the funding.

By the time Phase One is complete, we will have the strategic plan, the readiness picture, and the funder shortlist that drives everything forward. Phase Two is where we act on it.

01

Grant Intelligence and Opportunity Identification

Ongoing research across Australian foundations, government programs, and international funders, matched against your profile and priorities. You always know what is available and whether you are ready to go after it.

02

Grant Writing, End to End

Up to four grant applications per year, written from brief to submission-ready draft. Applications sourced through the partnership process. Additional applications beyond the four are available at a discounted rate.

03

Monthly Strategy and Pipeline Review

A 60-minute call each month covering the opportunity landscape, application progress, readiness developments, and pipeline planning. This is where we make decisions together about where to put the energy, what to pursue, and what to let pass.

04

Quarterly Strategic Review

Four times a year, a formal checkpoint to assess what has worked, recalibrate priorities, and adjust the funding calendar based on results and emerging opportunities. The 12-month funding calendar built in the first quarter becomes a living document: upcoming deadlines, preparation milestones, application windows, and acquittal dates, all in one place.

05

Grant Lifecycle Support

The work does not stop at submission. Readiness coaching continues throughout the partnership, building the evidence base, governance documentation, and organisational narrative needed to compete at a high level over time. For grants already won, impact reporting summaries and acquittal preparation guidance ensure obligations are met and position you for repeat funding from the same source.

On the four-application cap

The four-application cap is a capacity safeguard, not a ceiling on ambition. A single medium-complexity grant application takes 25 to 30 hours to write well. Four applications per year represents significant delivered value in writing alone, alongside all the scouting, coaching, strategy, and lifecycle work. Quality over volume is how applications win.

The investment

One partnership. One investment.

The Strategic Funding Partnership is a 12-month commitment. It covers both phases: the Strategic Foundations work and the full embedded partnership that follows. Up to four grant applications are included each year, alongside all the strategy, coaching, and lifecycle support that makes them worth submitting.

Why twelve months?

Twelve months may feel like a significant commitment upfront. Here is my case for why it matters.

The partnership has two phases for a reason. Phase One, the audit, the gap analysis, the strategy, is not overhead. It is the work that determines whether Phase Two delivers anything worth having. Submitting applications before we have built the evidence base, filled the readiness gaps, and developed the narrative for the full ecosystem would be a waste of your investment and, frankly, of the opportunity.

What you are looking for is a partner who takes responsibility for finding, preparing, and submitting applications that can actually win. The 12-month structure is what makes that possible. It gives us time to do Phase One properly, close the gaps, and then pursue a well-prepared pipeline, one application per quarter, each one submitted from a position of genuine readiness. That is the difference between submitting and winning.
